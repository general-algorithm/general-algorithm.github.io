EmbodiedPilot-1: Imagine What Will Happen Before Taking Drive Actions

July 27, 2024

We present a novel self-supervised and embodied generative framework for autonomous driving. Our approach emulates human-like behavior, which imagines what will happen in future scenarios before taking drive actions. Instead of only predicting labeled agents in supervised learning methods, we predict the whole world for trajectory planning.

The method, called EmbodiedPilot-1 (EP-1), extends trajectory planning by adding an imagine branch for predicting a high-quality future environment in parallel. EP-1 leverages an extensive dataset of 30k hours of high-quality driving footage to generate a state-of-the-art two-minute videos capable of autonomous driving. Moreover, without human annotations, EP-1 is capable of generating sufficiently accurate traffic map and plausible behaviors of traffic participants, indicating a comprehensive understanding of the information that requires annotation.

Overview

By observing others driving and then trying it oneself, any 17 year old can learn to drive in 20 hours of training, but current autonomous driving algorithm struggles in urban. We identify the cause of this issue as the information loss incurred by the supervised learning methods, e.g. inaccu- rate labeling precision and incomplete labeling categories definition by human. Our goal in this work is to develop a self-supervised embodied framework that learns like a hu- man.
